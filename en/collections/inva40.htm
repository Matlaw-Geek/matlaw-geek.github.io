IBM NetVista A40p

Quite underestimated computer from IBM.
The NetVista series existed between 2000 and 2004, replacing the PC 300 series, and later became the ThinkCentre series that still exists today.
This A40p model is fully equipped, i.e. Pentium III, RFID reader and fan on the front and a key lock on the case - as seen in the photo of the back of the computer. Admittedly, the floppy disk drive and the CD drive are not factory original (the floppy disk drive has a defective head and I am looking for parts).
It comes from Germany, so the BIOS was originally also in German. Fortunately, that was easy to fix.
It is currently used for everything related to Windows 9x. Hence the added GeForce 4 MX, SB Live! and 3Dfx Voodoo2 cards.
