Sony Ericsson K770

A representative of the K series, based on the DB2020 platform, the same as the more famous K550 and K800.

Like many Sony Ericsson cell phones, this model has features characteristic of the brand, such as a camera signed with the Cyber-shot logo and a reader for the (unfortunate) M2 memory cards.

The phone has a front camera, used only for video calls, and an LED that can be used to indicate, for example, a low battery. In addition, it offers blue illumination of the edges of the keys (used in camera mode), the stripes on the back cover and the icon near the shutter button.

Interestingly, the screen and lens are covered with glass instead of plastic, making them harder to scratch.

The biggest weaknesses of the K770, unfortunately, are the small screen and the paint on the keys, which wears off very easily.

This unit comes from the Polish Era network. It originally had operator's branding in software and a SIM lock, which was removed with an appropriate patch.
