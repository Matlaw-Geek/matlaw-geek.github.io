Samsung Galaxy Ace

The representative of the mid-range, although in 2011 its parameters slowly began to be fusty.

It was treated as a smaller version of the Galaxy S, but beyond a similar shape, appearance and system overlay had nothing in common. Along with the model Ace there were presented successively poorer variants: Gio, Fit, Mini.

I do not know whether the bigger disadvantage of this device is ridiculously small capacity of flash memory and RAM or its processor, which remembers the times... of the first Android phone. Seriously.
Because of the ARMv6 technology some applications simply don't work. Even in 2012, one of the mobile games ("Bad Piggies") did not support it.
The phone on the manufacturer's system has problems with smoothness and at times it happens to freeze. Which is quite surprising, because Android 2.3 should work very well.

However, knowing the weaknesses of this device and using a few tricks and tools, it is possible to use it, and its chassis is surprisingly pleasant in the hand.

Interestingly, the Galaxy Ace was used as a prop by Rowan Atkinson in the role of Mr. Bean, during the London 2012 Olympics opening ceremony.
