Dell Latitude E6430

The first Latitude with USB 3.0 ports and the last with the classic logo of this series.
Structurally and visually nearly identical to the predecessor (E6420), but the orange rim of the keyboard bezel has disappeared, as well as the plastic frames in the ports holes in the case.

Generally very nice and solid device, but the finish is very difficult to keep clean. Dust willingly sticks to the rubberized palmrest (and cleaning is nearly impossible), and the lid collects fingerprints and other marks like a magnet.

The keyboard comes from the E6440 model, hence the blue, not orange accents. In addition, compared to the factory equipment, the laptop was upgraded with buttons for the trackpoint (the factory keyboard lacked it) and an additional stick of 8 GB of RAM.
